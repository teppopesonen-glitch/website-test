About Me

Hi, I’m a 37-year-old adventurer and mountain enthusiast whose journey began back in 2014 with indoor climbing. What started as a simple hobby to stay active quickly evolved into a passion for pushing limits and exploring nature’s most awe-inspiring landscapes. Over the years, my love for travel and adventure has taken me across continents — from the rugged Andes to the icy peaks of the Arctic north and the dramatic slopes of the Caucasus mountains.

Each climb has taught me something new — not just about the mountains, but about myself. Huayna Potosi in Bolivia was my first 6000-meter summit and showed me what determination truly means. The Santa Cruz Trek in Peru deepened my respect for endurance and the simple beauty of life at high altitudes. From the volcanic ridges of Cotopaxi in Ecuador to the fjord-filled vistas of northern Norway, I’ve learned that every challenge is an opportunity to grow stronger, humbler, and more connected to the world.

My goal is simple — to become a better climber and adventurer every year. I’m not chasing records or fame, just the feeling of standing on a summit after a long, hard climb, knowing that I gave my best. Through this page, I hope to share my stories, inspire others to explore, and celebrate the power of perseverance and curiosity that the mountains awaken in all of us.
